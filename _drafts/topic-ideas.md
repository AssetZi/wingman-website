Blog Post Idea: “Fueling for Performance vs. Weight: How Wrestlers Should Build Their Plate”
This post would break down how wrestlers should adjust their nutrition depending on training intensity and goals using the “Athlete’s Plate” model—showing the difference between eating for hard training, moderate training, and weight management. It would focus on practical application: how to scale carbs up or down based on workload, how protein stays consistent, and how to manage fats without hurting performance. The angle should emphasize that most athletes underfuel on hard days and over-restrict on weight cuts, and this visual gives a simple system to fix both. Include real examples like what a practice-day plate vs. off-day plate actually looks like for a college wrestler.
